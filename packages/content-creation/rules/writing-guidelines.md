# Writing Guidelines

This rule establishes standards for creating high-quality written content that engages readers and achieves communication goals.

## Core Principles

1. **Clarity First** - Write clearly and simply
2. **Audience Focus** - Write for your target readers
3. **Purpose Driven** - Every content should have clear purpose
4. **Value Added** - Provide useful, actionable information

## Content Standards

### Structure & Organization
- Use clear, descriptive headings
- Follow logical flow: intro → body → conclusion
- Keep paragraphs under 5 sentences
- Use bullet points for lists and key information

### Language & Style
- Write in active voice (80%+ of content)
- Use simple, accessible language (8th-grade reading level)
- Avoid jargon and technical terms unless necessary
- Maintain consistent tone throughout

### Quality Requirements
- Minimum word count: 800 words for articles, 300 for blog posts
- Include at least 2 credible sources for factual content
- Use proper grammar, spelling, and punctuation
- Ensure factual accuracy with verification

### SEO Best Practices
- Include primary keyword in title and first paragraph
- Use secondary keywords naturally throughout
- Write compelling meta descriptions (150-160 characters)
- Use descriptive alt text for images

## Content Types

### Educational Content
- Focus on teaching and explaining
- Use step-by-step instructions
- Include examples and case studies
- Provide actionable takeaways

### Promotional Content
- Highlight benefits and value
- Use persuasive language
- Include clear calls-to-action
- Build trust and credibility

### Entertainment Content
- Use engaging storytelling
- Incorporate humor and personality
- Create emotional connections
- Encourage sharing and interaction

## Enforcement

These guidelines should be enforced through:
- Content review processes
- Editorial checklists
- Quality assurance workflows
- Regular content audits
